Looking to get Around Taniti

Transportation: Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air, though some arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week. Taniti is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. Taniti is in the process of expanding the airport so larger jets will be able to land on the island within the next few years. Ground Transportation: Public buses serve Taniti City and run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. every day. Private buses serve the rest of the island. Taxis are available in Taniti City, and rental cars can be rented from a local rental agency near the airport. Bikes and helmets are available to rent from several vendors (helmets are required by law). Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable. Many tourists stay in the area surrounding Merriton Landing: this area is easy to explore on foot.

Public and Private Buses

Getting around Taniti is easy and affordable thanks to its reliable public and private bus systems. Public buses serve Taniti City daily from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m., offering a convenient way for visitors to explore the city’s attractions, beaches, and shopping areas. For those venturing beyond the city, private buses provide transportation to other parts of the island, making it simple to reach remote sights and activities. Both options are budget-friendly and a great way to see more of Taniti without the need to rent a car.

Taxis

Taxis in Taniti offer a convenient and flexible way to get around, especially for visitors staying in or near Taniti City. Easily accessible and reasonably priced, taxis are a great option for reaching restaurants, hotels, attractions, and the airport without needing to navigate public transportation. Local drivers are often familiar with the best spots on the island and can provide helpful tips during your ride. Whether you’re heading out for a night on the town or catching an early flight, taxis provide dependable service when and where you need it.

Rental Cars

For visitors who want the freedom to explore Taniti at their own pace, rental cars are a convenient and flexible option. Available from a local agency near the airport, rental cars allow you to easily reach beaches, rainforest trails, and attractions across the island—including areas not served by public buses. Driving in Taniti is straightforward, and road signs are easy to follow. Whether you’re planning day trips around the island or simply want the convenience of your own transportation, renting a car is a great way to see all that Taniti has to offer on your own schedule.

Bikes

Exploring Taniti by bike is a fun, eco-friendly way to take in the island’s natural beauty. Bikes and helmets—required by law—are available for rent from several local vendors, especially near popular tourist areas. Taniti City is mostly flat and very walkable, making it perfect for casual rides through town, along the coastline, or to nearby attractions. For those staying in or around Merriton Landing, biking is a great way to get around without the need for public transportation or a rental car. It’s an affordable and enjoyable option for travelers of all ages.
